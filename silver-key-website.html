Common questions

Questions investors ask us

Straight answers to the most common questions before a strategy session.

What is an HMO and why is the yield so much higher?

A House in Multiple Occupation is let room-by-room to unrelated tenants who share kitchen and bathroom facilities. Because rent is collected per room rather than per property, gross yields typically run 7–10%, against 3–4% for a comparable single-let.

How much deposit do I need to get started?

It varies with your borrowing capacity, existing equity, and the deal structure. We work through this in your strategy session before recommending a specific property.

How long does the full process take, from acquisition to fully let?

Typically 4–6 months from exchange to a fully tenanted, licensed HMO, though timelines vary with local authority licensing and the scope of conversion works required.

Do you manage the finance, or do I need my own broker?

Either. We have a trusted lending network we can introduce you to, or we're happy to work alongside a broker you already use.

Can I invest through a limited company or SPV?

Yes. Many of our investors hold HMO assets through a limited company structure. We'll talk through the tax and lending implications with you and your adviser before you commit.

What makes Silver Key different from a buying agent or property manager?

A buying agent finds you a house; a property manager runs it once it's let. Silver Key does both, plus the finance structuring and the conversion itself: one team, accountable for the whole lifecycle.

What UK markets do you operate in?

We focus on regional cities with strong professional and key-worker room-share demand, established Article 4 clarity, and manageable licensing regimes, including Manchester, Liverpool, Leeds, Sheffield, and Newcastle.
