01 — The basics

What is an HMO, and why does it yield more?

A House in Multiple Occupation is let room by room to unrelated tenants who share kitchen and bathroom facilities. Because rent is collected per room rather than per property, gross yields typically run well ahead of a comparable single-let.

9.1%Silver Key HMO average
3.8%UK single-let average

The gap comes down to three structural factors:

Income per square foot. A six-bedroom HMO can generate the rental income of six separate tenancies from a single freehold, without six separate mortgages.

Diversified risk. One vacant room in a six-bedroom house is a partial void. One vacant single-let is a total void.

Structural demand. Regional UK cities face a persistent shortage of affordable, professionally let rooms for graduates, key workers, and young professionals priced out of one-bedroom flats.

07 — Due diligence

What to check before you commit

HMO conversion is more regulated than a standard buy-to-let. None of the below is exhaustive or a substitute for advice on a specific property, but it's where every conversation should start.

  • Article 4 Directions

    Some local authorities have withdrawn permitted development rights for small HMOs, meaning full planning permission is required even for a modest conversion. Always check with the local council before assuming a straightforward path.

  • Mandatory licensing

    Any HMO with five or more occupiers forming more than one household requires a mandatory licence, regardless of Article 4 status. Many councils also run additional or selective licensing schemes covering smaller HMOs.

  • Fire safety and room sizes

    Licensed HMOs must meet fire safety standards (interlinked alarms, fire doors, escape routes) and national minimum room size rules, which directly affect how many bedrooms a given floor plan can legally support.

  • Specialist finance

    Standard residential mortgages don't cover HMOs. A specialist HMO buy-to-let or commercial facility is needed, and lenders often require landlord experience or a larger deposit for larger properties.

  • Genuine local demand

    Verify room-share demand at the postcode level, not just the city average. A strong city-wide market can still have streets with weak demand for shared living.
